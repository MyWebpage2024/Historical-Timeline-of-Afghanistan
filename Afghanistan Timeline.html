Historical Timeline of Afghanistan

Timeline

Timeline

  • 500 B.C.: Darius I of Babylonia conquers the region.
  • 329 B.C.: Alexander the Great conquers the region.
  • 11th Century: Mahmud of Ghazni creates an empire stretching from Iran to India.
  • 13th Century: Genghis Khan invades Afghanistan.
  • 1700s: Afghanistan is united as a single country.
  • 1870: Islam takes root after invasions by various Arab conquerors.
  • 1921: Afghanistan gains independence following the Third British-Afghan War.
  • 1926: Amir Amanullah Khan declares Afghanistan a monarchy.
  • 1933: Zahir Shah becomes king, ruling for 40 years.
  • 1947: Pakistan is created, including a long border with Afghanistan.
  • 1953: Gen. Mohammed Daoud Khan becomes prime minister and seeks Soviet assistance.
  • 1956: Soviet Premier Nikita Khrushchev agrees to aid Afghanistan.
  • 1965: The Afghan Communist Party is formed.
  • 1973: Khan overthrows King Zahir Shah, establishing the Republic of Afghanistan.
  • 1978: Nur Mohammad Taraki becomes president after a communist coup.
  • 1979: USSR invades Afghanistan to support the communist regime.
  • 1982: 2.8 million Afghans flee to Pakistan, 1.5 million to Iran.
  • 1984: Osama bin Laden makes his first documented trip to Afghanistan.
  • 1986: Mujahadeen receives arms from the U.S., Britain, and China.
  • 1988: Osama bin Laden forms al-Qaida.
  • 1989: Peace accords signed; Soviet troops withdraw.
  • 1992: Mujahadeen ousts Dr. Mohammad Najibullah from power.
  • 1995: The Taliban rises to power, implementing strict Islamic laws.
  • 1997: The Taliban publicly executes Najibullah.
  • 1998: U.S. targets al-Qaida training camps in Afghanistan.
  • 2000: Bin Laden is widely believed to be hiding in Afghanistan.
  • March 2001: Taliban destroys Buddhist statues in Bamiyan.
  • Sept. 11, 2001: Al-Qaida orchestrates attacks on the World Trade Center and the Pentagon.
  • Oct. 7, 2001: U.S. and British forces begin airstrikes against Taliban targets.
  • Nov. 13, 2001: Northern Alliance enters Kabul.
  • Dec. 22, 2001: Hamid Karzai becomes leader of the interim government.
  • 2004: New constitution adopted; Hamid Karzai is elected president.
  • 2005: First parliamentary elections in over 30 years.
  • 2006: NATO expands peacekeeping to southern Afghanistan.
  • 2008: International community pledges $15 billion in aid.
  • 2009: U.S. announces a new strategy including additional troops.
  • 2011: Osama bin Laden is killed by U.S. forces.
  • 2014: Ashraf Ghani becomes president; U.S. begins reducing troops.
  • 2017: Trump commits to continued military involvement.
  • Feb. 2019: U.S. and Taliban sign peace deal.
  • Aug. 15, 2021: The Taliban takes over Kabul, leading to the collapse of the Afghan government.
  • Aug. 26, 2021: Suicide bombings at Kabul airport kill at least 169 Afghans and 13 U.S. troops.
  • Aug. 30, 2021: The U.S. completes its troop withdrawal from Afghanistan.
